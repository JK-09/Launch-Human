    Timeline of human space travel:

  • 12th April, 1961: First manned flight carrying Cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin (USSR).
  • 5th May, 1961: Alan Shepard became the first American in space during a fifteen minute sub-orbital flight.
  • July 21, 1961: The second US sub-orbital flight, reaching an altitude of 126 miles
  • August 6, 1961: Titov was the first to spend an entire day in space.
  • 1963: First woman in space is Valentina Tereshkova (USSR).
  • March 16,1966: Armstrong and Scott perform the first docking in space with another space vehicle - an Agena target rocket. The mission was prematurely terminated after only 10 hours and 41 minutes due to problems with a stuck thruster (NASA).
  • September 12, 1966: Gemini made the first American autopilot reentry and landing. A new record altitude was made of 850 miles (1360km). Gemini made a rendezvous and docking with a target Agena and Gordon made two spacewalks (NASA).
  • 1968: First piloted orbital mission of Moon (NASA).
  • October 11, 1968: The Apollo spacecraft makes its first flight as the astronauts perform several tests during its 11 days in orbit. The first live television pictures from space occurs on this mission.
  • 1969: First human on the Moon and first space launch from a celestial body by Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin (NASA).
  • 1971: First space station (USSR).
  • 1971: First moon car, the Lunar Rover, driven on the moon (NASA).
  • 1984: First untethered spacewalk by Bruce McCandless (NASA).
  • 1991: First British astronaut in space, Helen Sharman.
  • 1995: First Record for longest duration spaceflight (437.7 days) set by Valeri Polyakov (Russia).
  • 2000: Permanent staffing begins on International Space Station.
  • 2001: First tourist in space, Dennis Tito, which cost 20 million dollars for a week in orbit!
  • 2004: First privately funded manned spaceflight, by SpaceShipOne.
